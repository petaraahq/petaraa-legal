6. Gig Marketplace — Escrow & Delivery

The gig marketplace uses an escrow system to protect both buyers and sellers:

6.1 How It Works

  1. Buyer places order — payment is collected upfront via Razorpay.
  2. Payment held in escrow — the money is held securely. No order is confirmed without successful payment.
  3. Seller completes the work and submits delivery.
  4. Buyer reviews the delivery — the buyer can:
    • Approve — payment is released to the seller (minus Petaraa's commission).
    • Request revision — up to 2 revisions allowed per order.
    • Raise a dispute — Petaraa will review and make a fair decision.
  5. Auto-release: If the buyer does not respond within 5 days of delivery submission, payment is automatically released to the seller.

6.2 Delivery Extensions

If a seller cannot deliver on time:

  • The seller must communicate with the buyer via in-app chat.
  • If the buyer agrees, the seller can request a one-time deadline extension through Petaraa.
  • Only one extension per order is allowed, and it requires buyer approval.
  • If delivery is still not completed after the extension, the buyer is eligible for a full refund.

6.3 Disputes

In case of a dispute between buyer and seller, Petaraa will:

  • Review the case online based on evidence provided by both parties (chat history, delivered work, order details).
  • Make a fair and final decision — which may include full refund, partial refund, or release of payment.
  • No physical meeting or court involvement is required — all disputes are handled digitally through Petaraa.
